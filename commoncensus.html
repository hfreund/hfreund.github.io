Overview

Common Benefits for Individuals was one of the first online self service applications for benefit enrollment. It was forward thinking in capability, however, in order to stay competitive with a bustling marketplace after the passage of the Affordable Care Act, the entire user experience and user interface needed to be refreshed.

Existing Condition

Approach

Working with the development team, sales team, and client services team, we created an all new enrollment platform that met current needs of our clients while maintaining flexibility for future development we anticiapted would occure over the next 24-36 months. It was important to strike a balance between updating the UI to modern standards that would also dovetail with existing modules while not disrupting a user experience that many clients had become accustomed to.

Challenges

Although we had control of the underlying framework, the software has to integrate with over one hundred benefit enrollment modules from different insurance carriers, each with their own compliance requirements and many which were custom made before a time of flexible modules. Here we were severely restricted since almost any change would require compliance approval which could dramatically set the project off track.

Solution

The final solution was rolled out in phases to individual cases for user testing and feedback.
