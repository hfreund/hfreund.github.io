The Problem

The organization had expanded 7x since the original information architecture was laid down. The result was duplicated navigation structures, hyper-specific content buried down a navigation tunnel, and irrelevant navigation links.

Usability was low and maintenance costs were high. The process for adding content was tricky, prone to error, and generally not well-suited for the type of users. The result was a Baskin Robbins menu nearly 40 items long which was difficult to use on a desktop browser and practically impossible on mobile.
