Overview

Common Benefits for Individuals was one of the first online self service applications for benefit enrollment. It was forward thinking in capability, however, in order to stay competitive with a bustling marketplace after the passage of the Affordable Care Act, the entire user experience and user interface needed to be refreshed.

Existing Condition

Approach

Working with the development team, sales team, and client services team, we created an all new enrollment platform that met current needs of our clients while maintaining flexibility for future development. It was important to strike a balance between updating the UI to modern standards that would also dovetail with existing modules while not disrupting a user experience that many clients had become accustomed to.

User Flow

Challenges

Although we had control of the underlying framework, the software has to integrate with over one hundred benefit enrollment modules from different insurance carriers, each with their own compliance requirements and many which were custom made before a time of flexible modules. Here we were severely restricted since almost any change would require compliance approval which could dramatically set the project off track.

Administrative Web App Redesign

Common Benefits Administrator is a core piece of software for Common Census and it's been around for a long time. As the company worked out how this application would grow, I led the design exercise to help envision a bridge from present to future.

Administrative Mode

The majority of the application contains administrative functionality. Most of the features that had been added were poorly organized and buried deep inside of menus. This vision addressed the lack of a planned architecture and also createc a better framework for future feature development.

Administrative Mode

Interview Mode

The other side of this application is a one-on-one interview between an enroller and an individual either in person or over the phone. In this case, providing the best user experience meant improving the time each task takes for the enroller, and also reducing insider jargon for the enrollee.

Selling from the Website

A key objective in this build was to help the sales team move away from static PDF's which were time consuming to createa and often out of date within months. I worked with them to organize the website so they had all the information necessary to orchastrate a webinar. Additionally, the site was built so they could update content on the fly as more products and services became available.

Products & Pricing

Form to PDF

The sales team used a PDF case intake form that was difficult for clients to navigate and required frequent updates as services changed. By transitioning this form online, I gave the sales team a tool they could keep up to date so clients could submit complete case information. I also developed a custom generated PDF output that could be incorporated into the final contract.

Better Support Tools

The client services team had a good setup for phone support, but lacked an online knowledgebase for users to help themselves. I setup a knowledgebase in Help Scout and provided the team with a template that aligned with the company brand guidelines.

Brand Identity

The company last had a branding consultant in the early 2000's, which meant there was both a lack of brand consistency and the brand assets had not been redeveloped for current standards. I refined the previous style guidelines and expanded them to meet current web standards. I also rebuilt the logo and icon sets using SVG.
